Forging Our Path: Establishing the MyRewards Design System

Role

Project Owner

Responsibility

Design Ops

Product

MR Design System

Background

While there wasn't a dedicated guide for the MyRewards team, I took the initiative to build our design system and establish a clear design process. This empowers our team to contribute with greater efficiency and design integrity.

What I did

01. Starting with Existing Rules: Consolidating and Categorizing

I began by analyzing our current practices, identifying existing "rules of thumb" and their exceptions. This involved a comprehensive inventory and categorization of all text colors used across our interfaces.

02. Define Usage

With this initial understanding, I started to define the architecture of this design system and meticulously articulate the usage guidelines for each component.

For instance, I established rules for button sizes and hierarchy, and precisely defined various popup types along with their usage methods.

Crucially, I also integrated the similarities and differences in code implementation, ensuring our guidelines are practical for development. This entire effort serves as a practical resource for team members in their day-to-day work.

03. Building the system in Zeroheight

For this project, we're leveraging Zeroheight to build our design system. This meant a lot of images needed to be imported from Sketch.

(I'm also grateful to my colleague, D, for helping with image uploads, which accelerated the project.)
